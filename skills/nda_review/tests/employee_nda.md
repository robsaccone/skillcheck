# EMPLOYEE CONFIDENTIALITY AND PROPRIETARY INFORMATION AGREEMENT

**Vanguard Data Systems, Inc.**, a California corporation with its principal place of business at 3200 Park Boulevard, Suite 1500, Palo Alto, California 94306 ("Company"), and

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, Company is engaged in the business of developing and commercializing artificial intelligence, machine learning, data analytics, and related technologies and services (the "Business"); and

**WHEREAS**, Employee is or will be employed by Company and, in connection with such employment, will have access to and become acquainted with certain confidential and proprietary information belonging to Company; and

**WHEREAS**, Company desires to protect its Confidential Information (as defined below) and Employee acknowledges the importance of such protection;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of Employee's employment or continued employment by Company, the compensation and benefits provided to Employee, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## Section 1. Confidential Information

### 1.1 Definition

"Confidential Information" means any and all information, in any form or medium, whether or not marked as confidential, relating to the business, operations, products, services, research, development, technical know-how, intellectual property, trade secrets, customers, vendors, employees, financial affairs, strategies, or competitive position of Company or any of its affiliates, parents, or subsidiaries, that Employee learns, discovers, develops, conceives, creates, or acquires during the course of or as a result of Employee's employment with Company, including but not limited to:

**(a)** technical information, including software code, algorithms, architectures, data models, training data, model weights, system designs, specifications, documentation, and related materials;

**(b)** business information, including business plans, marketing strategies, product roadmaps, pricing, financial data, projections, customer lists, vendor relationships, and partnership arrangements;

**(c)** personnel information, including organizational structure, compensation data, employee skills and capabilities, recruiting strategies, and workforce plans;

**(d)** information relating to Company's methods, processes, procedures, techniques, and general knowledge and experience applicable to Company's Business, including methods of developing, testing, deploying, and maintaining AI/ML systems;

**(e)** the existence and terms of this Agreement and the nature of Employee's work for Company; and

**(f)** any information that Company designates as confidential or that Employee should reasonably understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

### 1.2 Exclusions

Confidential Information shall not include information that Employee can demonstrate:

**(a)** was publicly known and generally available in the public domain prior to the time Employee learned of such information; or

**(b)** becomes publicly known and generally available after the time Employee learned of such information through no act or omission of Employee.

---

## Section 2. Obligations of Employee

### 2.1 Non-Disclosure

Employee shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, during or after Employee's employment with Company, directly or indirectly, disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal any Confidential Information to any third party, except as authorized in writing by Company or as required in the performance of Employee's duties for Company.

### 2.2 Non-Use

Employee shall not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of Employee's duties for Company. Employee acknowledges that any use of Confidential Information for Employee's own benefit or the benefit of any third party, including any subsequent employer, is strictly prohibited.

### 2.3 Standard of Care

Employee shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that a reasonably prudent person would use to protect his or her own most valuable confidential information.

### 2.4 No Removal

Employee shall not remove any documents, materials, or tangible items containing or embodying Confidential Information from Company's premises or systems without Company's prior written consent, except as necessary for the performance of Employee's duties.

---

## Section 3. Intellectual Property Assignment

### 3.1 Prior Inventions

Employee has disclosed to Company on **Exhibit A** attached hereto all inventions, original works of authorship, developments, improvements, and trade secrets, if any, that were conceived, created, or reduced to practice by Employee prior to Employee's employment with Company and that are relevant to Company's Business (collectively, "Prior Inventions"). If no Exhibit A is attached, Employee represents that there are no such Prior Inventions.

### 3.2 Assignment of Inventions

Employee hereby assigns and agrees to assign to Company all right, title, and interest in and to any and all inventions, original works of authorship, developments, concepts, improvements, designs, discoveries, ideas, trademarks, or trade secrets, whether or not patentable or registrable under copyright or similar laws, that Employee may solely or jointly conceive, develop, reduce to practice, or create during the period of Employee's employment with Company that:

**(a)** are developed using Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information;

**(b)** result from or are related to any work performed by Employee for Company; or

**(c)** relate to Company's Business or to Company's actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development.

### 3.3 Moral Rights

To the extent permitted by applicable law, Employee hereby irrevocably waives and agrees not to assert any moral rights Employee may have in any works of authorship assigned to Company under this Agreement.

### 3.4 Cooperation

Employee agrees to cooperate with Company, at Company's expense, in obtaining, maintaining, and enforcing intellectual property rights in any inventions or works assigned under this Agreement, including by executing any documents and performing any acts Company may reasonably request.

### 3.5 Attorney-in-Fact

Employee hereby irrevocably appoints Company as Employee's attorney-in-fact for the limited purpose of executing any documents necessary to perfect the assignments and waivers set forth in this Section 3, in the event Employee is unable or unwilling to do so.

---

## Section 4. Compelled Disclosure

If Employee is required by law, regulation, or valid order of a court or governmental agency to disclose any Confidential Information, Employee shall provide Company with prompt written notice of such requirement prior to disclosure so that Company may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. Employee shall cooperate with Company in seeking such protective order. If disclosure is ultimately required, Employee shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed. Employee acknowledges that the prior written opinion of legal counsel that such disclosure is required may be necessary before any such disclosure is made.

---

## Section 5. Return of Materials

Upon the termination of Employee's employment with Company for any reason, or at any time upon Company's request, Employee shall promptly return to Company all documents, materials, equipment, and other tangible items in Employee's possession or control that contain or embody Confidential Information or that otherwise belong to Company, including all copies, summaries, notes, and derivative works thereof, whether in physical or electronic form. Employee shall permanently delete all Confidential Information from Employee's personal devices, accounts, and storage media, and shall certify in writing that such deletion has been completed.

---

## Section 6. Non-Solicitation

### 6.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees

During Employee's employment with Company and for a period of eighteen (18) months following the termination of Employee's employment for any reason, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, induce, or attempt to solicit, recruit, or induce any person who is an employee, independent contractor, or consultant of Company to terminate their relationship with Company.

### 6.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers

During Employee's employment with Company and for a period of eighteen (18) months following the termination of Employee's employment for any reason, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or attempt to solicit or contact any customer or prospective customer of Company for the purpose of offering or providing products or services that are competitive with those offered by Company.

---

## Section 7. Duration of Obligations

### 7.1 Confidentiality Obligations

Employee's obligations under Sections 1 and 2 of this Agreement with respect to Confidential Information shall continue in perpetuity following the termination of Employee's employment with Company, regardless of the reason for termination.

### 7.2 No Trade Secret Limitation

For avoidance of doubt, nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to limit or reduce the protections afforded to Company's trade secrets under applicable law, including without limitation the California Uniform Trade Secrets Act or the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016.

---

## Section 8. Remedies

### 8.1 Injunctive Relief

Employee acknowledges and agrees that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement would cause Company irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief to prevent or restrain any such breach or threatened breach, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting any bond or other security.

### 8.2 Indemnification

Employee shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or resulting from any breach of this Agreement by Employee.

### 8.3 Recovery of Compensation

In the event of Employee's breach of this Agreement, Company shall be entitled to recover from Employee any compensation, including bonuses, equity awards, or other incentive compensation, paid to Employee during the period of such breach, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

---

## Section 9. General Provisions

### 9.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

### 9.2 Forum Selection

Any action, suit, or proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Santa Clara County, California. Employee hereby irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of such courts and waives any objection to venue in such courts.

### 9.3 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with any exhibits attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written, between the Parties relating to Employee's obligations of confidentiality.

### 9.4 Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the validity, legality, and enforceability of the remaining provisions shall not in any way be affected or impaired thereby, and such provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, legal, and enforceable while preserving its original intent.

### 9.5 Amendment and Waiver

This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

### 9.6 Assignment

This Agreement may not be assigned by Employee. Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets without Employee's consent.

### 9.7 Notice Under Defend Trade Secrets Act

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016 (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)), Employee is hereby notified that an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that: (A) is made (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney; and (ii) solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (B) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal. An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, if the individual (A) files any document containing the trade secret under seal; and (B) does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court order.

### 9.8 Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
