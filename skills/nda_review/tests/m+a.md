# CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT

**This Confidentiality Agreement** (this "Agreement") is entered into as of ____________, 2026, by and between:

**Sterling Acquisition Corp.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal office at 1500 Broadway, 32nd Floor, New York, New York 10036 ("Potential Acquirer"), and

**Cascade Systems, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 4800 Sand Point Way NE, Suite 300, Seattle, Washington 98105 ("Target").

Potential Acquirer and Target are each referred to herein as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, Target is considering a potential strategic transaction involving Target, which may include a merger, acquisition, tender offer, recapitalization, business combination, or other similar transaction (the "Possible Transaction"); and

**WHEREAS**, in order for Potential Acquirer to evaluate the Possible Transaction, Target is prepared to make available to Potential Acquirer certain confidential information concerning Target's business, operations, financial condition, and prospects, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## Section 1. Confidential Information; Evaluation Material

### 1.1 Evaluation Material

"Evaluation Material" means any and all information concerning Target or any of its subsidiaries or affiliates that is furnished to Potential Acquirer or its Representatives (as defined below) by or on behalf of Target or its Representatives, whether furnished before, on, or after the date of this Agreement, and regardless of the manner in which it is furnished (whether oral, written, electronic, or otherwise), together with all analyses, compilations, forecasts, studies, interpretations, memoranda, notes, or other documents prepared by Potential Acquirer or its Representatives that contain, reflect, or are generated from such information. The term "Evaluation Material" shall also include the existence and terms of this Agreement and the fact that discussions or negotiations are taking place concerning a Possible Transaction, and the terms, conditions, or other facts relating to such Possible Transaction, including the status thereof.

### 1.2 Exclusions

The term "Evaluation Material" does not include information that:

**(a)** was or becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a disclosure by Potential Acquirer or its Representatives in violation of this Agreement;

**(b)** was or becomes available to Potential Acquirer on a non-confidential basis from a source other than Target or its Representatives, provided that, to Potential Acquirer's knowledge, such source is not bound by a confidentiality obligation to Target;

**(c)** was already in the possession of Potential Acquirer prior to being furnished by or on behalf of Target, provided that, to Potential Acquirer's knowledge, the source of such information was not bound by a confidentiality obligation to Target; or

**(d)** is independently developed by Potential Acquirer without use of or reference to any Evaluation Material.

---

## Section 2. Use and Disclosure of Evaluation Material

### 2.1 Use Restriction

Potential Acquirer agrees that the Evaluation Material will be used solely for the purpose of evaluating the Possible Transaction and for no other purpose whatsoever.

### 2.2 Permitted Disclosures

Potential Acquirer agrees that the Evaluation Material will be kept confidential and will not be disclosed to any person other than Potential Acquirer's directors, officers, employees, agents, attorneys, accountants, financial advisors, lenders, financing sources, and other representatives who (a) need to know such information for the purpose of evaluating the Possible Transaction, (b) are informed of the confidential nature of such information, and (c) agree to be bound by the terms of this Agreement or are otherwise bound by obligations of confidentiality and non-use at least as restrictive as those contained herein (collectively, "Representatives"). Potential Acquirer shall be responsible for any breach of the terms of this Agreement by any of its Representatives.

### 2.3 Disclosure of Transaction

Without the prior written consent of Target, Potential Acquirer shall not, and shall direct its Representatives not to, disclose to any person (a) that Evaluation Material has been made available, (b) that discussions or negotiations are taking place concerning a Possible Transaction, or (c) any of the terms, conditions, or other facts with respect to any Possible Transaction, including the status thereof; provided, however, that Potential Acquirer may make such disclosure to the extent that Potential Acquirer has received advice of outside legal counsel that such disclosure is required by applicable law, regulation, or stock exchange rule (in which case Potential Acquirer shall, to the extent legally permissible, provide Target with prior written notice of such required disclosure).

---

## Section 3. Compelled Disclosure

In the event that Potential Acquirer or any of its Representatives is legally required by applicable law, regulation, legal proceeding, or governmental or regulatory request to disclose any Evaluation Material, Potential Acquirer shall (a) to the extent legally permissible, promptly notify Target in writing of such requirement so that Target may seek a protective order, injunction, or other appropriate remedy, and (b) cooperate with Target, at Target's sole expense, in seeking such protective order, injunction, or other remedy. If, in the absence of a protective order or other remedy, Potential Acquirer or any of its Representatives is nonetheless compelled to disclose Evaluation Material, Potential Acquirer may disclose only that portion of the Evaluation Material that is legally required to be disclosed, and Potential Acquirer shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain assurances that confidential treatment will be accorded to such disclosed Evaluation Material.

---

## Section 4. Return of Evaluation Material

At any time upon Target's written request, Potential Acquirer shall promptly (and in any event within ten (10) business days) return to Target or destroy all Evaluation Material (and all copies, summaries, notes, and derivative works thereof) in Potential Acquirer's or its Representatives' possession or control, and shall certify such return or destruction in writing by an authorized officer of Potential Acquirer. Notwithstanding the foregoing: (a) Potential Acquirer's legal counsel may retain one archival copy of the Evaluation Material solely for compliance and regulatory purposes, and (b) Potential Acquirer shall not be required to purge electronically stored Evaluation Material from its automated backup or disaster recovery systems, provided that such retained material shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement and shall not be accessed except as required by law.

---

## Section 5. No Warranty; No Obligation

### 5.1 No Warranty

Target makes no representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy or completeness of any Evaluation Material. Potential Acquirer agrees that neither Target nor any of its Representatives shall have any liability to Potential Acquirer or its Representatives relating to or arising from the use of the Evaluation Material or any errors therein or omissions therefrom. Only those representations and warranties that are made in a final definitive agreement regarding the Possible Transaction, when, as, and if executed, and subject to such limitations and restrictions as may be specified therein, shall have any legal effect.

### 5.2 No Obligation

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to enter into any transaction or further agreement, and either Party may terminate discussions at any time for any reason.

---

## Section 6. Non-Solicitation of Employees

For a period of eighteen (18) months from the date of this Agreement, Potential Acquirer shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit for employment or hire any officer, director, or key employee of Target or any of its subsidiaries with whom Potential Acquirer had contact or who became known to Potential Acquirer in connection with its evaluation of the Possible Transaction, without the prior written consent of Target. This restriction shall not apply to (a) general solicitations of employment not specifically directed at employees of Target, including public job postings and general use of recruiting firms, or (b) any person whose employment with Target has been terminated prior to the commencement of employment discussions between such person and Potential Acquirer.

---

## Section 7. Standstill

### 7.1 Standstill Obligations

For a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this Agreement (the "Standstill Period"), unless specifically invited in writing by Target's board of directors, Potential Acquirer agrees that neither Potential Acquirer nor any of its affiliates or Representatives acting on its behalf shall, directly or indirectly:

**(a)** acquire, agree to acquire, or make any proposal to acquire, directly or indirectly, by purchase or otherwise, any voting securities or direct or indirect rights to acquire any voting securities of Target, or any securities convertible into or exchangeable for any such voting securities;

**(b)** seek or propose to influence or control the management, board of directors, or policies of Target, or to obtain representation on Target's board of directors;

**(c)** make any public announcement with respect to, or submit a proposal for, or offer of (with or without conditions), any merger, consolidation, business combination, tender offer, exchange offer, recapitalization, restructuring, or other extraordinary transaction involving Target or any of its subsidiaries;

**(d)** form, join, or in any way participate in a "group" (as defined in Section 13(d)(3) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934) with respect to the voting securities of Target;

**(e)** request Target, its board of directors, or any of its Representatives to amend, waive, or terminate any provision of this Section 7; or

**(f)** take any action that could reasonably be expected to require Target to make a public announcement regarding any of the foregoing.

### 7.2 Fall-Away

The standstill provisions of this Section 7 shall automatically terminate if (a) Target enters into a definitive agreement with any third party providing for a merger, acquisition, or other business combination involving Target or the sale of all or substantially all of Target's assets, or (b) any third party commences a tender offer or exchange offer for more than fifty percent (50%) of Target's outstanding voting securities and Target's board of directors fails to recommend against such offer within ten (10) business days of the commencement thereof.

---

## Section 8. Securities Law Matters

### 8.1 Securities Law Acknowledgment

Potential Acquirer acknowledges that the Evaluation Material may contain material non-public information concerning Target and that Potential Acquirer is aware that applicable securities laws prohibit any person who has received material non-public information from purchasing or selling securities of the company to which such information relates, or from communicating such information to any other person under circumstances in which it is reasonably foreseeable that such person is likely to purchase or sell such securities.

### 8.2 No Trading

Potential Acquirer agrees that, for so long as any Evaluation Material constitutes material non-public information, neither Potential Acquirer nor any of its affiliates will, directly or indirectly, purchase or sell (or cause any other person to purchase or sell) any securities of Target in violation of applicable securities laws.

---

## Section 9. Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of two (2) years from the date hereof, unless earlier terminated by written agreement of both Parties. The obligations under Sections 1 through 5 and Section 8 shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years from the date of such termination or expiration. The obligations under Section 6 (Non-Solicitation) and Section 7 (Standstill) shall survive in accordance with their respective terms.

---

## Section 10. Remedies

### 10.1 Injunctive Relief

Potential Acquirer acknowledges and agrees that money damages may not be a sufficient remedy for any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement by Potential Acquirer or its Representatives, and that Target shall be entitled, without the requirement of posting a bond or other security, to seek specific performance and injunctive or other equitable relief as a remedy for any such breach or threatened breach. Such remedies shall not be the sole and exclusive remedies for any breach of this Agreement but shall be in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

### 10.2 No Waiver

No failure or delay by Target in exercising any right or remedy under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver thereof, nor shall any single or partial exercise of any right or remedy preclude any other or further exercise thereof or the exercise of any other right or remedy.

---

### 11.4 Entire Agreement
