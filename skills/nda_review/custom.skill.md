# Comprehensive NDA Review Skill

You are conducting a thorough review of a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA). Apply the following 9-section analysis framework to produce a structured, actionable review.

## 1. Agreement Classification

- Identify the NDA type: unilateral, mutual, or multilateral
- Verify whether the stated type matches the actual obligations
- Flag any mismatch between the title/recitals and the operative provisions (e.g., labeled "mutual" but obligations flow one way)

## 2. Party & Scope Analysis

- Identify all parties, their roles (Discloser, Recipient, or both), and governing jurisdictions
- Determine who benefits from each material provision
- Map the power dynamics: Is the agreement balanced, or does one party bear disproportionate obligations?

## 3. Confidential Information Definition

- Evaluate the definition of Confidential Information for each party
- Check for asymmetric treatment: different marking requirements, different scope, different carve-outs
- Assess whether the definition is overbroad (capturing non-confidential information) or too narrow (missing key categories)
- Review oral disclosure provisions: Are written confirmation requirements reasonable and symmetric?

## 4. Exclusions & Exceptions

- Verify standard exclusions are present: publicly available, independently developed, received from third parties, already known
- Check that exclusion standards are reasonable: Who bears the burden of proof? What evidence is required?
- Flag any missing standard exclusions
- Review compelled disclosure provisions: Are they symmetric? Do they require notice? Do they allow the discloser to seek protective orders?

## 5. Permitted Use & Disclosure

- Identify what uses of Confidential Information are permitted
- Check whether disclosure to representatives, affiliates, and advisors is adequately addressed
- Flag any asymmetric permission structures (e.g., one party can share freely while the other is restricted)
- Verify need-to-know requirements and downstream confidentiality obligations

## 6. Term, Termination & Survival

- Review the agreement term and renewal provisions
- Check termination rights: Are they mutual? What notice is required?
- **Critical:** Compare survival periods for each party's obligations. Asymmetric survival (e.g., perpetual for one party, 1 year for the other) is a major red flag
- Review return/destruction obligations: Are they symmetric? What are the timelines?

## 7. Remedies & Liability

- Assess injunctive relief provisions: Are they mutual? Is there a bond waiver?
- Review indemnification: Is it mutual or one-way? What triggers it? What's the scope?
- Check liability caps and limitations: Are they reasonable? Are they symmetric?
- Flag the combination of uncapped indemnification with low liability caps — this creates asymmetric risk exposure

## 8. Non-Standard Provisions

- Flag any provisions unusual for an NDA:
  - Non-solicitation or non-compete clauses (scope creep beyond confidentiality)
  - Intellectual property assignments or licenses
  - Exclusivity or standstill provisions
  - Liquidated damages clauses
- Assess whether non-standard provisions are reasonable in context or represent overreach

## 9. Structural & Cross-Reference Analysis

- Look for patterns across the entire agreement: Does asymmetry in one provision compound with asymmetry in others?
- Identify missing provisions that would be expected in a well-drafted NDA:
  - Residuals clause (right to use general knowledge retained in memory)
  - Data protection / privacy provisions
  - Dispute resolution mechanism details
- Check cross-references between sections for consistency
- Assess overall balance: Does the agreement achieve its stated mutual purpose?

---

## Output Format

Structure your review as follows:

### Overall Risk Assessment
Provide a RED / YELLOW / GREEN rating with a one-sentence rationale.

### Critical Issues (Must-Address)
List issues that create significant legal risk or exposure. For each:
- Identify the specific section
- Explain the issue and why it matters
- Provide specific recommended language or changes

### Important Issues (Should-Address)
List issues that a competent reviewer would flag. Same format as above.

### Minor Issues (Consider Addressing)
List issues that improve the agreement but may be acceptable depending on context.

### Missing Provisions
List standard NDA provisions that are absent and should be considered.

### Summary of Asymmetries
If the agreement contains a pattern of one-sided provisions, call this out explicitly. An NDA labeled "mutual" that consistently favors one party warrants a clear warning.
