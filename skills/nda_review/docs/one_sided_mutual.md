# MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of ________________, 2026 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

**Acme Technologies, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at 1200 Innovation Drive, Suite 400, Wilmington, Delaware 19801 ("Acme" or the "Disclosing Party"); and

**[Company Name]**, a ________________ [state] [entity type], with its principal place of business at ________________ ("Recipient" or the "Receiving Party").

Acme and Recipient are each referred to herein as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship (the "Purpose") and, in connection therewith, each Party may disclose to the other certain confidential and proprietary information; and

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to establish the terms and conditions under which such confidential information will be disclosed, received, and protected;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. PURPOSE

1.1 This Agreement governs the disclosure of Confidential Information between the Parties in connection with exploring, evaluating, and potentially entering into a business relationship related to technology integration, product development, or strategic partnership opportunities (the "Purpose").

1.2 Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to disclose any particular Confidential Information or to enter into any further agreement.

---

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

2.1 **Acme's Confidential Information.** "Confidential Information" of Acme means all information, in any form or medium, disclosed by or on behalf of Acme to Recipient, whether before or after the Effective Date, that relates to Acme's business, technology, products, services, customers, partners, operations, finances, strategies, or affairs, including without limitation: (a) trade secrets, inventions, patents, patent applications, copyrights, and other intellectual property; (b) technical data, research, development plans, product roadmaps, software, algorithms, source code, and system architectures; (c) business plans, financial information, projections, marketing strategies, pricing, customer lists, and vendor relationships; (d) employee information, organizational structure, and personnel data; and (e) any analysis, compilation, study, or other material prepared by Recipient that contains, reflects, or is derived from any of the foregoing. All information disclosed by Acme shall be deemed Confidential Information regardless of whether it is marked, designated, or otherwise identified as confidential.

2.2 **Recipient's Confidential Information.** "Confidential Information" of Recipient means information disclosed by Recipient to Acme that is (a) clearly and conspicuously marked as "Confidential" or "Proprietary" at the time of disclosure, or (b) if disclosed orally or visually, is identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and summarized in writing, marked as confidential, and delivered to Acme within five (5) business days following such oral or visual disclosure.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

3.1 **Standard of Care.** Each Party agrees to hold the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence and to protect it using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

3.2 **Restrictions on Use.** Neither Party shall use the other Party's Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose.

3.3 **Restrictions on Disclosure.** Neither Party shall disclose the other Party's Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except as expressly permitted herein.

3.4 **Acme's Permitted Disclosures.** Acme may disclose Recipient's Confidential Information to its officers, directors, employees, agents, advisors, consultants, affiliates, subsidiaries, parent companies, potential investors, potential acquirers, and their respective representatives (collectively, "Acme Representatives") who have a need to know such information for the Purpose. Acme shall inform Acme Representatives of the confidential nature of such information but shall not be required to obtain written confidentiality agreements from Acme Representatives prior to disclosure.

3.5 **Recipient's Permitted Disclosures.** Recipient may disclose Acme's Confidential Information only to its employees who (a) have a direct need to know such information solely for the Purpose, (b) have executed written confidentiality agreements with terms no less restrictive than this Agreement prior to receiving any Confidential Information, and (c) are identified by name on a list provided to and approved by Acme in advance. Recipient shall be directly liable for any breach of this Agreement by any person to whom Recipient discloses Acme's Confidential Information.

---

## 4. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) was publicly known and generally available in the public domain prior to the time of disclosure by the Disclosing Party, or becomes publicly known and generally available after disclosure through no wrongful action or inaction of the Receiving Party;

(b) was already in the lawful possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as established by documentary evidence predating the disclosure that was created in the ordinary course of business and not in anticipation of this Agreement;

(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as established by contemporaneous written records created in the ordinary course of business by individuals who had no access to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

(d) is rightfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party with respect to such information.

---

## 5. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

5.1 **Recipient's Obligation.** If Recipient becomes legally compelled by judicial or administrative process, regulatory authority, or by applicable law or regulation to disclose any of Acme's Confidential Information, Recipient shall provide Acme with prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) so that Acme may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or other remedy is not obtained, Recipient shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain assurance that confidential treatment will be afforded to the disclosed information. Recipient shall cooperate fully with Acme (at Recipient's sole expense) in connection with any efforts by Acme to seek such protective order or other remedy.

5.2 **Acme's Rights.** For the avoidance of doubt, Acme shall have no obligation to provide notice to Recipient in the event Acme is compelled to disclose Recipient's Confidential Information and may disclose such information as required without liability to Recipient.

---

## 6. RETURN AND DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

6.1 **Recipient's Obligations.** Upon the written request of Acme or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Recipient shall promptly (and in any event within ten (10) business days): (a) return to Acme all originals, copies, and reproductions of Acme's Confidential Information in any form or medium; (b) destroy all notes, analyses, compilations, studies, memoranda, and other documents that contain, reflect, or are derived from Acme's Confidential Information; and (c) certify in writing, signed by an officer of Recipient, that all such materials have been returned or destroyed.

6.2 **Acme's Rights.** Acme may retain Recipient's Confidential Information for a reasonable period following termination or expiration of this Agreement for archival, audit, and compliance purposes, and shall have no obligation to return or destroy such information.

---

## 7. WARRANTIES AND DISCLAIMERS

7.1 **Mutual Warranty.** Each Party warrants that it has the right to disclose the Confidential Information it provides to the other Party under this Agreement.

7.2 **Recipient's Acknowledgment.** Recipient acknowledges and agrees that all Confidential Information of Acme is provided "AS IS" and that Acme makes no representations or warranties, express or implied, regarding the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for a particular purpose of any Confidential Information. Recipient agrees that Acme shall have no liability to Recipient resulting from Recipient's use of or reliance on any Confidential Information.

---

## 8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of two (2) years, unless earlier terminated. Acme may terminate this Agreement at any time upon fifteen (15) days' written notice to Recipient. Recipient may terminate this Agreement upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to Acme, subject to Acme's written consent.

8.2 **Survival of Acme's Information.** Recipient's obligations with respect to Acme's Confidential Information shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement in perpetuity or until such time as Acme's Confidential Information no longer qualifies as confidential information, whichever occurs later.

8.3 **Survival of Recipient's Information.** Acme's obligations with respect to Recipient's Confidential Information shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of one (1) year following the date of termination or expiration.

---

## 9. REMEDIES AND LIABILITY

9.1 **Injunctive Relief.** Recipient acknowledges and agrees that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement by Recipient may cause Acme irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, Acme shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity, without the necessity of proving actual damages, posting any bond, or other security.

9.2 **Indemnification.** Recipient shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Acme, its officers, directors, employees, agents, and affiliates (collectively, the "Acme Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs) arising out of or resulting from: (a) any breach or alleged breach of this Agreement by Recipient or its employees, agents, or representatives; (b) any unauthorized use or disclosure of Acme's Confidential Information; or (c) any negligent or wrongful act or omission of Recipient in connection with this Agreement.

9.3 **Limitation of Liability.** IN NO EVENT SHALL ACME'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY TO RECIPIENT UNDER OR IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT EXCEED ONE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($1,000.00), REGARDLESS OF THE FORM OF ACTION OR THEORY OF LIABILITY. THIS LIMITATION SHALL APPLY EVEN IF ACME HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

9.4 **No Consequential Damages.** IN NO EVENT SHALL ACME BE LIABLE TO RECIPIENT FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, PUNITIVE, OR EXEMPLARY DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO DAMAGES FOR LOSS OF PROFITS, GOODWILL, DATA, OR OTHER INTANGIBLE LOSSES, ARISING OUT OF OR IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT.

---

## 10. NON-SOLICITATION

10.1 During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years following its termination or expiration, Recipient shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, or attempt to hire any employee, contractor, or consultant of Acme, or induce or attempt to induce any such person to leave the employ or engagement of Acme.

10.2 The restrictions set forth in Section 10.1 shall apply regardless of whether the Recipient became aware of such employee, contractor, or consultant through the disclosure of Confidential Information or through any other means during the term of this Agreement.

10.3 In the event of a breach of this Section 10, Recipient shall pay to Acme, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, an amount equal to one hundred percent (100%) of the annual compensation of each employee, contractor, or consultant solicited or hired in violation of this Section.

## 11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

11.2 **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware. Recipient hereby irrevocably submits to the personal jurisdiction of such courts and waives any objection to venue or inconvenient forum. Each Party shall bear its own costs and attorneys' fees in connection with any such dispute, except that in any action by Acme to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

11.3 **Assignment.** Recipient shall not assign or transfer this Agreement or any of its rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of Acme, and any attempted assignment without such consent shall be null and void. Acme may freely assign this Agreement to any affiliate, successor, or assignee without notice to or consent from Recipient.

11.4 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

11.5 **Amendment.** This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

11.6 **Waiver.** No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right, power, or privilege hereunder shall operate as a waiver thereof.

11.7 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. The Parties agree to negotiate in good faith a substitute provision that most nearly achieves the original intent of the invalid or unenforceable provision.

11.8 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same agreement. Electronic signatures shall be deemed original signatures for all purposes.

11.9 **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when delivered personally, sent by confirmed email, or sent by nationally recognized overnight courier to the addresses set forth above.
